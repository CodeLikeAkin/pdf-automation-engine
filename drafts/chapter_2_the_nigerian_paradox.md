# Chapter 2: The Nigerian Paradox: Rethinking the "Swallow"

In Nigeria, a meal isn't a "meal" without a swallow. Whether it’s Eba, Pounded Yam, Fufu, or Semovita, these staples are the soul of our culinary identity. However, for those managing high blood pressure and diabetes, they represent a paradox: our comfort food has become a metabolic burden.

### The Glycemic Trap
The "swallow" is almost pure starch. When we pound yam or ferment cassava, we break down the fibers, making it extremely easy for the body to convert that starch into glucose. 
- **Pounded Yam**: This has a High Glycemic Index (GI). It hits the bloodstream like a shot of pure sugar.
- **Garri (Eba)**: While a beloved staple, it is highly processed and causes rapid insulin spikes.

### The Heart-Healthy Rotation
You don't have to give up swallows, but you must change the *type* and the *portion*.
1.  **Acha (Fonio)**: The ancient grain of the North. It is low-GI, high-fiber, and has a wonderful texture.
2.  **Unripe Plantain Flour**: Rich in "resistant starch," it actually helps your gut health and keeps your sugar steady.
3.  **Oatmeal Swallow**: A world-standard for heart health. High in beta-glucan fiber that cleans out your arteries.

### The "Ugu" Rule
Whatever swallow you choose, the soup must be the hero. Fill half your bowl with leafy greens (*Ugu, Ewedu, Oha*). The fiber in the vegetables creates a "shield" that slows down the sugar from the swallow.

In this guide, we aren't taking away your culture; we are giving you a more resilient way to enjoy it.
