# Legal Research: Citizen-Police Interaction (Nigeria)

## 1. Fundamental Human Rights (1999 Constitution)
- **Section 34**: Right to Dignity of Human Person (No torture or degrading treatment).
- **Section 35**: Right to Personal Liberty.
- **Section 37**: Right to Privacy (Specifically mentions "privacy of their correspondence, telephone conversations and telegraphic communications").
- **Section 39**: Right to Freedom of Expression (Foundation for the Right to Record).

## 2. The Stop & Search Framework (Police Act 2020)
- **Section 49-54**: Rules governing stop and search.
- **The "Reasonable Suspicion" Threshold**: Must be objective grounds (not just a "hunch").
- **Mandatory Disclosures (Officer)**:
    - Name and Rank.
    - Police Station/Command.
    - The **Object** of the search (what are they looking for?).
    - The **Grounds** for the search (why they suspect you have it).
- **The Profiling Ban (Section 54)**: It is unlawful to search based on:
    - Hairstyle (Dreadlocks, tinted hair, etc.).
    - Manner of dress (Sagging, "luxury" fashion).
    - Previous convictions.
    - Stereotypes of groups (e.g., "Young men with laptops").

## 3. Digital Privacy & Device Searches
- **Constitutional Protection**: Section 37 covers digital devices.
- **Search Warrants**: A search of a phone or laptop generally requires a warrant from a Magistrate or Court.
- **IGP Directives**: Multiple circulars (2020-2023) ban indiscriminate phone searches at checkpoints. Officers can only search devices if they are part of a specific investigation with a warrant or clear link to a reported crime.

## 4. The Right to Record
- **Legal Basis**: Section 39 of the Constitution.
- **Key Ruling**: Federal High Court, Warri (2026) affirmed that citizens can record officers in public performing duties.
- **Police Position**: Force PRO has stated multiple times that "filing or photographing officers on duty is not an offense."
- **Condition**: Must not obstruct or physically interfere with the officer's duty.

## 5. Arrest & Detention (ACJA 2015)
- **Notification**: Must inform the suspect of the reason for arrest immediately.
- **Right to Counsel**: Right to a lawyer before making a statement.
- **Bail is Free**: Section 31 (ACJA) and Section 62 (Police Act 2020) emphasize that bail is free for bailable offenses.
- **Arrest by Proxy**: Section 7 of ACJA 2015 explicitly prohibits arresting anyone in place of a suspect.
